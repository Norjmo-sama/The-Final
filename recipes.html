Cranberry Baked Brie

Warm, gooey brie wrapped in flaky pastry with tart cranberries and rich brown sugar—this is a guaranteed crowd-pleaser.


Serves: 8-12


Ingredients:

  • 1 round Brie cheese (8–12 oz)
  • 1 sheet puff pastry, thawed
  • ½ cup cranberry sauce (whole berry works best)
  • 2–3 tbsp brown sugar
  • 1 egg (for egg wash)

Steps:

  • Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  • Line a baking sheet with parchment paper or a silicone mat.
  • Wrap brie in puff pastry
  • Lightly flour your counter.
  • Roll the puff pastry out slightly to remove creases.
  • Place the brie in the center of the pastry.
  • Spoon cranberry sauce + brown sugar on top
  • Spoon cranberry sauce directly on top of the brie.
  • Sprinkle evenly with brown sugar.
  • Add optional nuts, herbs, or orange zest for extra depth.
  • Wrap the Brie
  • Fold the corners of the puff pastry up and over the brie.
  • Pinch gently to seal.
  • Flip seam-side down onto the baking sheet.
  • Egg Wash for Golden Color
  • Whisk the egg with 1 tbsp water.
  • Brush over the entire pastry for a glossy golden crust.
  • Bake at 375°F for 20–25 min
  • Pastry is deep golden brown
  • Cheese inside is soft and molten
  • If the pastry browns too fast, loosely tent with foil for the last 5 minutes.
  • Rest & Serve
  • Let sit 5–10 minutes before cutting (prevents cheese overflow).

Serve warm with:

  • Crackers
  • Sliced apples or pears
  • Toasted baguette slices
Deviled Eggs

Classic, creamy, and always the first appetizer to disappear—these traditional deviled eggs are a Christmas Day staple.


Serves: 8-10


Ingredients:

  • 12 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 teaspoon yellow mustard
  • 1 teaspoon white vinegar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • Optional garnish: paprika or chopped parsley

Steps:

  • Place eggs in a large saucepan and cover with cold water. Bring to a boil. Once boiling, remove from heat, cover, and let sit for 12 minutes.
  • Drain hot water and transfer eggs to an ice bath. Let cool completely, then peel.
  • Slice eggs in half lengthwise and carefully remove yolks. Place yolks in a bowl and mash with a fork until smooth.
  • Add mayonnaise, mustard, vinegar, salt, and pepper to the yolks. Mix until creamy and smooth.
  • Spoon or pipe the filling back into the egg whites. Sprinkle lightly with paprika or parsley.
Honey Glazed Christmas Ham

Sweet, sticky, and beautifully caramelized—this honey-glazed spiral ham is the centerpiece every Christmas table deserves.


Serves: 8-12


Ingredients:

  • 1 bone-in spiral-cut ham about 10 pounds
  • 1 cup honey
  • 1/2 cup firmly packed brown sugar
  • 1 teaspoon McCormick® Ground Ginger
  • 1 teaspoon McCormick® Ground Mustard
  • 1/2 teaspoon McCormick® Ground Cinnamon

Steps:

  • Preheat oven to 325°F. Place the ham on its side in roasting pan. Mix honey, brown sugar and spices in small bowl until well blended. If mixture is too thick, microwave on HIGH 30 seconds to 1 minute or until smooth, stirring after 30 seconds.
  • Brush 1/2 of the honey mixture over ham, gently separating the slices so mixture can reach middle of ham. Cover loosely with foil.
  • Bake 1 hour, basting occasionally with pan drippings. Remove foil. Brush with remaining honey mixture. Bake 45 minutes longer. Serve ham with pan drippings.
Prim Rib

Tender, juicy, and coated with a savory herb crust—this classic prime rib is the centerpiece of an unforgettable Christmas dinner.

Serves: 8-12


Ingredients:

  • 1 bone-in prime rib roast (8–10 pounds)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil or softened butter
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh rosemary
  • 2 tablespoons chopped fresh thyme
  • 2–3 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 2 teaspoons coarse black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder (optional)
  • 1 cup beef broth

Steps:

  • Preheat oven to 450°F. Remove prime rib from refrigerator 1–2 hours before cooking to bring to room temperature. Pat dry with paper towels. In a small bowl, mix olive oil, garlic, rosemary, thyme, salt, pepper, and onion powder until well blended. Rub the mixture evenly over the entire roast.
  • Place prime rib fat-side up on a rack in a roasting pan. Pour beef broth into the bottom of the pan. Roast uncovered at 450°F for 20 minutes to sear. Reduce oven temperature to 325°F and continue roasting about 13–15 minutes per pound for medium-rare to medium doneness.
  • Remove roast from oven and tent loosely with foil. Let rest 20–30 minutes before slicing. Serve with pan drippings.
Garlic Mashed Potatoes

Creamy, rich, and full of comforting garlic flavor—these mashed potatoes are the perfect side for any Christmas dinner.


Serves: 8–10


Ingredients:

  • 5 pounds russet or Yukon gold potatoes, peeled and cubed
  • 6 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • Optional: sour cream or cream cheese

Steps:

  • Place potatoes and garlic in a large pot and cover with cold water. Add a pinch of salt. Bring to a boil and cook 15–20 minutes, or until potatoes are fork-tender. Drain well.
  • In a small saucepan, warm the butter and heavy cream over low heat until melted and hot, but not boiling.
  • Return potatoes and garlic to the pot. Mash until smooth. Pour in the warm butter and cream mixture. Add salt and pepper. Mash until creamy. Stir in sour cream or cream cheese if using.
  • Taste and adjust seasoning. Serve hot.
Greenbean Casserole

Classic, creamy, and topped with a crispy onion crunch—this timeless holiday side dish never goes out of style.


Serves: 8–10


Ingredients:

  • 3 cans (14.5 oz each) cut green beans, drained
  • 1 can (10.5 oz) cream of mushroom soup
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce/li>
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 1/2 cups crispy fried onions, divided
  • Optional: 1/2 cup shredded cheddar cheese

Steps:

  • Preheat oven to 350°F. In a large bowl, combine cream of mushroom soup, milk, soy sauce, and black pepper. Stir until smooth.
  • Add the drained green beans and 1 cup of the crispy fried onions to the mixture. Stir until evenly coated. If using cheese, mix it in at this step.
  • Pour mixture into a greased 9x13 baking dish. Bake uncovered for 25 minutes.
  • Remove from oven and sprinkle the remaining 1/2 cup of crispy fried onions over the top.
  • Return to oven and bake an additional 5–10 minutes, until bubbly and golden on top.
